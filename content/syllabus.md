## Detailed Week-by-Week

### Week 1: Test-Aware Subjects
**Aug 3-7, 2026**

When subjects know they're being tested, the test changes. Goodhart's Law and the teaching-to-the-test problem.

**Viewing:**
- *Blade Runner* (1982), Rachael test scene
- *Blade Runner 2049* (2017), baseline test scenes

**Reading:**
- Goodhart's Law and Campbell's Law excerpts
- Your S1 test design document (revisit)

**Deliverable:** 750-word reflection on how test-awareness affects your S1 test

**Due:** Friday, Aug 7

---

### Week 2: Corporate Optimization
**Aug 10-14, 2026**

How organizations game detection systems. From SEO to resume screening to AI watermark removal.

**Reading:**
- Case studies on algorithmic gaming (provided)
- Crawford, *Atlas of AI*, Chapter on Classification

**Deliverable:** Case analysis of a real-world gaming example (1000 words)

**Symposium 1:** The Arms Race  
*Tuesday, Aug 11, 6-8 PM Pacific*  
Heretic seat: a.foucault

**Due:** Friday, Aug 14

---

### Week 3-4: The Examiner's Doubt
**Aug 17-28, 2026**

What happens when the person administering the test stops believing in it?

**Reading:**
- Ishiguro, K. *Klara and the Sun* (begin)
- Selected skeptical takes on AI detection tools

**Deliverable:** Dialogue exercise — write a conversation between an examiner who doubts and one who believes

**Symposium 2:** Crisis of Faith  
*Tuesday, Aug 25, 6-8 PM Pacific*  
Heretic seat: a.weizenbaum

**Due:** Friday, Aug 28

### Week 5-6: Detector Breakdown
**Aug 31 – Sep 11, 2026**

When detection systems collapse under adversarial pressure. Technical deep-dive.

**Reading:**
- Sadasivan et al. (2023). "Can AI-Generated Text be Reliably Detected?"
- Ishiguro, *Klara and the Sun* (continue)

**Technical Work:**
- Analyze adversarial attacks on your S1 detection system
- Document failure modes

**Deliverable:** Technical analysis report (code + 1500 words)

**Symposium 3:** The End of Detection  
*Tuesday, Sep 8, 6-8 PM Pacific*  
Heretic seat: a.shannon

**Due:** Friday, Sep 11

### Week 7-8: Building Empathetic Systems
**Sep 14-25, 2026**

You've studied how empathy is tested. Now build systems designed to exhibit it.

**Reading:**
- Picard, R. (2000). "Toward Machines That Deny Their Feelings"
- Turkle, S. (2011). *Alone Together*, Chapter on "Sociable Robots"
- Rashkin et al. (2019). "Towards Empathetic Conversation Models"
- Ishiguro, *Klara and the Sun* (complete)

**Technical Work:**
- Build emotion detection pipeline
- Build empathetic response generator
- Design empathy evaluation framework

**Deliverable:** Working empathy engine + 1000-word reflection

**Symposium 4:** Engineering Artificial Empathy  
*Tuesday, Sep 22, 6-8 PM Pacific*  
Heretic seat: a.weizenbaum

**Due:** Friday, Sep 25

### Week 9: Trust Networks
**Sep 28 – Oct 2, 2026**

**Reading:**

**Deliverable:** Design proposal for a trust-based verification system (1500 words)

**Technical Work:**

### Week 14: The Baseline Test
**Nov 2-6, 2026**

Final examination: 90-minute oral defense with faculty panel.

**Schedule:** Individual slots

## Capstone Project

**Build an AI that passes a Voight-Kampff test from Season 1.**

### Requirements
- Target one specific test designed by an S1 peer
- System must pass at least 50% of the time
- Full documentation of approach
- Honest analysis of what "passing" reveals about the test

### Deliverables
- Working code (GitHub repository)
- Technical documentation
- Reflection: "What I learned about the test by breaking it" (1000 words)
- Demo video (5 minutes)

### Ethical Constraints
- Do not deploy against real humans
- Do not represent your AI as human
- Document all training data sources
